Types of Coffees

Irish coffee has four main ingredients: coffee, Irish whiskey, sugar and cream. But there are many variations of this classic coffee drink, some of which include steamed milk. While the traditional Irish coffee consists of hot coffee, Irish whiskey, sugar, and a layer of cream on top, creative variations have emerged to cater to diverse tastes. One such variation involves the addition of steamed milk, which imparts a different dimension to the classic concoction. The steamed milk contributes to a smoother experience.

A flat white is a blend of micro-foamed milk poured over a single or double shot of espresso. This micro foam is steamed milk infused with air, which creates tiny air bubbles, to create a smooth and creamy taste. The combination of micro-foamed milk and espresso in a flat white results in a velvety texture and a well-balanced flavor profile. The micro foam adds a luxurious, creamy quality to the drink, enhancing the overall mouthfeel. The key to a perfect flat white lies in the art of steaming the milk, as it requires precision to achieve the ideal temperature and consistency for the micro foam. The smooth integration of the espresso and micro foam creates a harmonious blend, where the rich, bold notes of the coffee are complemented by the subtle sweetness and silkiness of the milk. This popular coffee beverage has gained a following for its ability to deliver a strong coffee flavor with a luscious, velvety finish, making it a favorite among espresso enthusiasts who appreciate a well-crafted and satisfying drink.

Instant coffee is made from real coffee beans. The soluble and volatile contents of the beans are extracted. Then the water is removed so powder or concentrated soluble coffee powder are left over. This process of transforming real coffee beans into instant coffee ensures the preservation of the soluble and volatile elements responsible for the rich flavor and aroma of coffee. The resulting powder or concentrated soluble coffee retains the essence of the coffee beans, allowing for a convenient and rapid preparation method. Instant coffee has become a popular choice for individuals seeking a quick and efficient way to enjoy a cup of coffee without the need for elaborate brewing processes. Its versatility, long shelf life, and easy solubility in hot water make it a convenient option for coffee enthusiasts around the world, catering to various preferences and lifestyles.

Djibouti, nestled in the Horn of Africa, is not only known for its strategic location at the crossroads of Africa and the Middle East but also for its unique and flavorful coffee. The coffee industry in Djibouti contributes significantly to the country's economy, and Djibouti coffee holds a distinct place in the world of specialty coffee. The coffee production in Djibouti is characterized by the cultivation of Arabica beans in the highlands. The favorable altitude and climate contribute to the development of beans with a nuanced flavor profile, often featuring bright acidity, floral notes, and a medium to light body. Djibouti's coffee farmers take pride in their traditional cultivation methods, emphasizing sustainable practices that respect the environment.
